⚑ Peak hour

05:00 is the busiest hour by a clear margin — 282 calls, about 3.0× the 24-hour average of 94. 06:00 (201) and 09:00 (182) follow — pre-shift startup checks and gas-flush calibration cluster right around shift change.

⚑ Thursday spikes, Monday stays quiet

Thursday is the busiest weekday over the full history — 386 calls, about 20% above the 7-day average of 322.4, edging out Tuesday (373, +16%). Monday remains the quietest by a wide margin at just 247, roughly 23% below average and 34% below Tuesday. Worth a look at whether Monday coverage/reporting is thinner, not that Monday's machines are healthier.

⚑ Days lead

Days shift (06:00–18:00) carries 67% of all call-outs (1519 of 2257); Nights carries the remaining 33% (738).

⚑ Busiest line

Line 3 takes the most call-outs — 282, about 1.7× Line 9's count (170), the lowest-volume line.

⚑ Mechanical beats oxygen still

Mechanical-failure language — "seal" (151), "sensor" (175), "belt" (177), "blade" (40), "knife" (2) — totals 545 mentions, more than "flush" + "gas" combined (448), a gap of 97 mentions (about 22% more). Across the full history, this is still a mechanical-wear problem as much as a calibration one.

⚑ Biggest lever

74% of call-outs (1659 of 2257) closed with no root cause logged at all — the single biggest opportunity to actually cut repeat calls.
